The Accounting Lifecycle

Hover over steps to highlight the separation of concerns between ILP Nodes and Settlement Engines.

1

Clearing (Packet Flow)

As ILP Fulfill packets are routed from Peer B to Peer A, Peer A incurs a debt. Both nodes instantly update their internal Clearing Balances. No real money has moved yet.

2

Settle Threshold Hit

Peer A's balance for Peer B drops below a configured settle_threshold. Peer A decides it must pay Peer B to continue peering.

3

Trigger Settlement Engine

Peer A's ILP Node sends an API request to its local Settlement Engine (SE) instructing it to settle the specific amount owed to Peer B.

4

Ledger Transfer

Peer A's SE executes a transaction on the underlying ledger (e.g., Ethereum) transferring real value to Peer B's SE account.

5

Receipt & Notification

Peer B's SE monitors the ledger, detects the incoming funds, and makes an API call back to Peer B's ILP Node acknowledging receipt.

6

Balance Credited

Peer B updates its internal Clearing Balance, zeroing out Peer A's debt. Trust is restored, and high-speed packet routing continues.
