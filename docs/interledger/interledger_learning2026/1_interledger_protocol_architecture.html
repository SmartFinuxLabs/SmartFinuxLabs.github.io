ILP Payment Lifecycle

Hover over steps to highlight the protocol layer routing and underlying ledger settlements.

1

Setup & Addressing

The Receiver generates a cryptographic condition (hash) and shares it, along with their ILP Address, with the Sender (usually out-of-band via SPSP).

2

ILP Prepare Packets

The Sender constructs an ILP Prepare packet containing the condition and amount. Connectors route this packet hop-by-hop toward the Receiver.

3

Conditional Holds (Ledgers)

Simultaneously, peers execute conditional transfers on the underlying Ledgers. Funds are locked (held) pending the release of the cryptographic preimage.

4

ILP Fulfill (The Preimage)

The Receiver validates the packet and replies with an ILP Fulfill packet containing the fulfillment (preimage). This packet travels back exactly the way it came.

5

Ledger Settlement

As the Fulfill packet is routed back, Connectors use the revealed preimage to execute/claim the conditional holds on their respective underlying ledgers, settling the funds.
