# Q&A

## What are you building?*

We are building **Verity**, an on-chain supply chain finance platform for invoice factoring and settlement. Verity helps suppliers turn buyer-accepted receivables into financeable assets, lets buyers validate and confirm payable obligations, and enables investors to fund accepted invoices through a transparent USDC escrow workflow. The core idea is that an invoice alone should not be financeable; only buyer-confirmed **Due Value** should become the basis for funding, settlement, yield, and payment history.

## Pitch what you're building in 1 paragraph.*

Verity modernizes supply chain finance by converting messy invoice workflows into a verifiable value-resolution process. A supplier creates an invoice, the buyer reviews the commercial evidence and accepts the payable, the platform prevents duplicate financing through deterministic invoice hashing, and investors can fund the accepted receivable with clear yield and risk terms. Settlement then happens through USDC escrow, distributing supplier advances, investor principal, yield, fees, and residual balances with an auditable record. This gives SMEs faster working capital, buyers stronger invoice control, and investors access to buyer-confirmed real-world receivables.

## When did you start working on this startup? Why did you choose this idea?*

We started formally working on Verity in May 2026, after exploring how accounting truth, invoice acceptance, and payment settlement break apart across real supply chain workflows. We chose this idea because SMEs often need working capital before buyers pay, but invoice financing is still exposed to weak invoice evidence, duplicate financing risk, slow reconciliation, and fragmented settlement rails. Our insight is that the financeable object should not be a raw invoice; it should be buyer-confirmed Due Value, backed by evidence, acceptance, audit history, and programmable settlement.

## What other companies are solving this problem today or could if they wanted to? Why will you succeed against them?*

Traditional supply chain finance and invoice financing companies include Taulia, C2FO, PrimeRevenue, Tradeshift, Demica, SAP Ariba, banks, and factoring providers. Newer blockchain or stablecoin-adjacent approaches include companies such as Skyscend, InvoFi, Zenia, and other receivables or trade finance platforms. Most competitors start from financing, ERP integration, or payment automation. Verity starts one layer earlier: resolving whether a commercial claim has become an accounting-valid obligation. We believe we can win by making buyer acceptance, duplicate prevention, invoice state history, USDC escrow, yield calculation, and verifiable payment profiles part of one workflow, instead of treating financing and settlement as separate tools bolted onto invoices.

## What’s your current traction?*

We are at MVP stage. We have defined the product thesis, the Value Resolution Layer, the core user flows for suppliers, buyers, and investors, the frontend technical requirements, the MVP implementation roadmap, and fourteen use cases covering invoice creation, buyer validation, duplicate prevention, factoring, investor funding, USDC escrow settlement, delinquency handling, yield display, and payment profile building. We also have a product presentation and HTML deck for the Verity concept. Our next traction milestone is an end-to-end working demo where a supplier creates an invoice, a buyer accepts Due Value, an investor funds it, and escrow distributes settlement at maturity.
